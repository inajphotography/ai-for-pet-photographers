# Quarterly Market Research Prompts
## 10 Prompts to Stay Sharp on Your Local Market
### For Pet Photographers — by Ina J Photography

Your market changes. New photographers open up. Dog parent communities shift. New questions emerge.

Run these 4 times a year. Takes about 20 minutes. Keeps your positioning sharp.

---

## HOW TO USE THESE

Open Claude Code in any folder and paste the prompt. Claude will use your CLAUDE.md for context.

Run the full set quarterly, or dip into individual prompts whenever you need a specific answer.

## DOG PARENT INTELLIGENCE

### Prompt 1: Local Community Pulse Check

```
I'm a pet photographer based in [your town].

Search for active dog owner communities, Facebook groups, and events in [your town] and surrounding area.

Tell me:
- Which communities seem most active right now (names, approximate size if available)
- Any local events or markets where dog parents gather
- Any shifts in what dog parents in this area seem to be talking about or caring about

Be specific to [your town]. If you can't find much, note that and tell me what you did find.

Summarise in bullet points. Keep it practical.
```

### Prompt 2: What Dog Parents Are Asking Right Now

```
Search for the most common questions dog parents and pet photography clients are asking online right now.

Use Reddit, Quora, Facebook groups, Google autocomplete patterns, and any other sources you can access.

I want to know:
- What questions they're asking before booking a pet photographer
- What concerns or hesitations come up most
- What they wish they knew before their first pet photography session
- What they're saying after a session (good and bad)

Give me 10 to 15 specific questions with brief notes on where you found them.
```

### Prompt 3: Content Gap Finder

```
I'm a pet photographer in [your town]. Read my CLAUDE.md for context on my business and ideal client.

Search for blog posts, Instagram content, and YouTube videos about pet photography in [your town] and [state/region].

Tell me:
- What topics have already been covered well
- What topics seem underrepresented or poorly answered
- What my ideal client is searching for that doesn't have a good answer yet

Give me 5 specific content ideas I could create that would fill real gaps. Be specific about the title and the angle.
```

## COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE

### Prompt 4: Local Competitor Landscape Update

```
Search for pet photographers currently active in [your town] and within 50km.

For each one you find, note:
- Their name and business name
- Their main positioning or message (from their website or Instagram bio)
- Price signals if visible
- What they seem to specialise in or lead with
- When they last posted (active vs dormant)

Give me a list of 5 to 10 photographers with brief notes on each.

At the end, tell me:
- What most of them have in common (the "everyone says this" problem)
- Where I could differentiate based on my CLAUDE.md profile
```

### Prompt 5: Competitor Content Audit

```
Search for Instagram and Facebook content from pet photographers in [your town] posted in the last 3 months.

Tell me:
- What types of content they're posting most (behind the scenes, final images, reels, tips, etc.)
- What topics keep coming up across multiple photographers
- What's performing well (high engagement) vs. what's getting ignored
- What's missing — what nobody in [your town] seems to be creating

Give me a practical summary with 3 to 5 content opportunities I could act on.
```

### Prompt 6: Pricing Signals Research

```
Search for pet photographer pricing in [your town] and [state].

Tell me:
- What pricing is visible publicly (starting rates, packages, what's included)
- What the common range seems to be at the low, mid, and high end
- How photographers are framing their pricing (by session, by package, value-based, etc.)
- Any pricing language that comes up a lot

Note: I'm not trying to race to the bottom. I want to understand the market landscape.

Read my CLAUDE.md to understand my current offer. After the research, tell me one thing worth considering based on what you found.
```

## CONTENT STRATEGY

### Prompt 7: Trending Topics in Pet Photography

```
Search for what's trending in pet photography content right now — globally and in Australia (or [your country]).

Tell me:
- What topics are getting the most engagement on Instagram and TikTok in the pet photography niche
- Any new types of sessions or styles gaining momentum
- What pet photography educators and coaches are talking about
- Any industry shifts or conversations happening in the last 3 months

Give me 5 content ideas or topics I could lean into based on what's trending.
```

### Prompt 8: Keyword and Search Intent Research

```
I'm a pet photographer in [your town]. Read my CLAUDE.md for context.

Search for what potential clients are typing into Google when they're looking for a pet photographer in [your town] or [state].

Tell me:
- The most common search phrases
- The intent behind those searches (ready to book vs. just researching)
- Any local search opportunities that seem underutilised
- What my website should say to show up for these searches

Give me a practical list of 10 to 15 search phrases and brief notes on each.
```

## IDEAL CLIENT RESEARCH

### Prompt 9: Dog Parent Demographics Deep Dive

```
Research the dog owner demographics in [your town] or [state/region].

I want to know:
- Rough age range of dog owners who are most active on social media
- What breeds are most popular in [your town] (if available)
- What dog parents in this area seem to spend money on
- What they care about beyond the dog (lifestyle, home, family, etc.)

Use any sources available — census data, pet industry reports, social media research, local news.

Connect this back to my CLAUDE.md ideal client profile. Tell me if my current ICA sounds accurate, or if the research suggests I should adjust my targeting.
```

### Prompt 10: Voice of Customer Mining

```
Search for reviews, comments, and posts from pet photography clients who have shared their experience online.

Use Google reviews, Facebook, Instagram comments, Reddit, and any other sources you can access.

Tell me:
- The exact words and phrases clients use when they describe a great pet photography experience
- What they say moved them most — what they didn't expect to feel
- What common fears or hesitations came up before they booked
- What they wish they'd known beforehand
- Any specific language that keeps appearing across multiple reviews

Give me 15 to 20 direct quotes or paraphrases from real clients.

This language should go into my website, my captions, and my follow-up emails.
```

## SAVE YOUR FINDINGS

After running any of these prompts, ask Claude:

```
Based on the research you just completed, write me 3 action items I could act on this week.
Be specific. No vague suggestions.
```

Then save the output in a dated file or your notes system. When you run the research next quarter, compare what changed.
