## Step 1: The Questions

Once they type `1`, say:

**Step 1. This is the part that makes the guide real.**

I'm going to ask you 5 quick questions about how you actually work. Your answers go straight into the guide. No generic content. No "insert your process here" placeholders.

Read their CLAUDE.md first. Pull what you already know about their offer and process so you don't ask things you already know. Then ask only what's missing.

Ask the questions ONE AT A TIME. Wait for each answer before asking the next.

The 5 questions:

1. **"How far in advance do you typically need clients to book? And what's the best way for someone to lock in a date with you?"**

2. **"When you think about the sessions that go really well, what did those clients do to prepare? What should they bring, wear, and think about beforehand? And is there anything they should NOT do?"**

3. **"Walk me through what happens during the session itself. What's your rough process? How long does it usually run? What should they expect from you on the day?"**

4. **"What happens after the session? How do you deliver the images, when do they see them, and what are their options for getting artwork?"**

5. **"If you could give one piece of advice to a first-time pet photography client, what would it be? The thing you wish every client knew before they arrived."**

After question 5, say:

**Perfect. That's everything I need.**

This is the part where it all comes together.
